# ART BY CONSTANZA ESPEJO

### FROM TEXT TO TRUTH

In the vibrant framework of the Ibero American Film Festival Miami, the local art scene experienced one of its most intimate and significant days with the official presentation of the book Meisner Technique + Combined Resources: From Text to Truth by actress and director Constanza Espejo. A work destined to become essential reading for performers and creators who seek to work from authenticity, organicity and stage presence.

The evening was organized by We Art Foundation, which brought together artists, filmmakers, students and art lovers for a meeting that combined literature, film and collective reflection. The event also included a special screening of Después del Final, followed by an exclusive Q\&A with its director Pablo César and part of the cast, creating an intimate atmosphere where the audience could dialogue directly about creative processes and artistic sensibilities.

The spirit of the meeting was clear: to celebrate two projects in conversation with each other. On the one hand, a cinematographic work that reflects on the human bond and its emotional dimension, and on the other, a book that proposes a renovating method to reach the scenic truth without resorting to the emotional sacrifice of the interpreter. An evening that was experienced as a bridge between arts, generations and views.

The book, From Text to Truth, proposes a profound journey through the Meisner Technique, revealing its true complexity and distancing it from the reduced vision that tends to limit it only to repetition exercises. Espejo invites us to understand it as a complete path towards truth on stage, where emotion is not forced, but emerges inevitably from a genuine encounter with the other.

One of the most revolutionary contributions of the book is the turn towards a healthier, modern and sustainable training. The author proposes to leave behind the emotional memory, so often exhausting and potentially harmful, to embrace a technique that starts from the neutrality of the text, active listening and bodily presence. From there, emotion appears alone, clean, without artifice.

With years of experience on stage and in international workshops, Espejo develops an accessible, clear and proven method. He modernizes the Meisner technique by integrating it with contemporary directing and staging resources, proposes tools to optimize rehearsals without losing artistic depth and offers strategies for directors to accompany without imposing, achieving livelier and more organic scenes. Each page functions as a practical guide to authenticity.

More than a manual, the book is positioned as a true manifesto of acting renewal. It is a bridge between tradition and new ways of training, acting and directing, providing a current, human and transforming language. It is a work that ignites life on stage and protects the emotional health of the actor, at the same time as it enhances his or her creative freedom.

The meeting at the Ibero American Film Festival Miami confirmed what was already intuited: Constanza Espejo's work arrives to mark a new stage in Spanish-speaking training. A fresh, lucid and necessary proposal for the contemporary art scene. For those who wish to delve into this creative universe, the book is already available.
