**Miami Freedom Park**

Inter Miami CF is entering a new chapter with the construction of Miami Freedom Park, a pioneering project that will reshape the city’s landscape and redefine the football experience in South Florida. Located next to Miami International Airport, the development blends sport, entertainment and community spaces in an ambitious vision that reflects Miami’s energy and diversity.

At the heart of Miami Freedom Park will be a 25,000-seat soccer-specific stadium, the permanent home of Inter Miami CF. This state-of-the-art venue will offer a first-class experience with advanced technology, premium hospitality areas and vibrant supporter zones that celebrate both the club and Miami’s multicultural identity.

Beyond the pitch, Miami Freedom Park will span 131 acres with restaurants, shops, offices and a hotel. The design aims to turn the area into an all-day destination for residents and visitors, not just on match days. A highlight is the creation of the Jorge Mas Canosa Park, a 58-acre public green space that will bring new life and recreation opportunities to the community.

The project also includes youth soccer fields, open plazas and entertainment spaces for cultural events, festivals and outdoor activities. This reflects Inter Miami’s commitment to community development and to fostering the next generation of players and fans across South Florida.

Thanks to its strategic location by the airport, the complex will offer unmatched accessibility for both local supporters and international travelers. Visitors arriving in Miami will be greeted immediately by the stadium’s silhouette, a new symbol of the city’s sporting passion and growing global influence.

For Miami, Freedom Park represents more than a sports facility. It is a major urban transformation. This privately financed project is expected to create thousands of jobs, attract tourism and boost the local economy while repurposing underutilized land into new public spaces.

Inter Miami’s ownership group, led by David Beckham and entrepreneur Jorge Mas, envisions the development as a legacy project that anchors the team in the heart of the city and strengthens Miami’s identity as an international destination for culture and sport.

When it opens in 2026, Miami Freedom Park will not only give Inter Miami CF a permanent home, it will also stand as a new gateway to the city where football, entertainment and community come together under a bold vision for the future.
